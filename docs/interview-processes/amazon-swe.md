## Unique Elements

### Bar Raiser System
Amazon's Bar Raiser is a unique mechanism to maintain hiring quality:
- **Who**: Senior employees with special interview training
- **Role**: Independent evaluator not from the hiring team
- **Power**: Can veto any hire
- **Purpose**: Ensure every hire raises the average quality
- **Identity**: Not revealed to candidates

**Confidence:** âœ… (Well-documented Amazon practice)

### Leadership Principles Integration
Unlike other companies that separate technical and behavioral:
- LP questions appear in EVERY round
- Even coding rounds include 2-3 behavioral questions
- Data-driven answers expected (include metrics)
- STAR format essential

**Confidence:** âœ… (Consistent across all sources)

### Writing Culture
- Amazon is famous for its writing culture
- 6-page memos instead of PowerPoints
- Clear, data-driven communication valued

**Confidence:** âœ…

## Preparation Tips

1. **Leadership Principles**: Memorize all 16, prepare 2-3 STAR stories for each
2. **Data-Driven Answers**: Include metrics and numbers in every behavioral answer
3. **STAR Format**: Situation, Task, Action, Result - essential for LP questions
4. **Coding**: Focus on medium LeetCode, emphasize clean, optimal solutions
5. **Bar Raiser**: Prepare your best stories - this is the most important interview
6. **Customer Focus**: Frame answers around customer impact when possible

## Common Mistakes

- Under-preparing for behavioral questions (biggest differentiator vs other companies)
- Not including metrics/data in LP answers
- Treating coding rounds as purely technical (LP questions will come)
